A Guardian of the Thar Desert's Wildlife & Great Indian Bustard Conservation

A Story of Passion, Wildlife Protection & Desert Safari Expertise

Nestled in the heart of Jaisalmer's golden expanse in Rajasthan, Radheshyam Pemani Bishnoi developed an unbreakable bond with the wilderness from a young age. Growing up amidst the sweeping sands of the Thar Desert, his passion for wildlife safaris and bird watching, particularly the critically endangered Great Indian Bustard (Ardeotis nigriceps), took root naturally. Witnessing the bird's plight against habitat destruction and human-induced threats in Desert National Park, Radhe channeled his love for nature into a lifelong commitment to wildlife conservation and eco-tourism.


As an expert wildlife guide at the Desert National Park in Jaisalmer, Radhe turned his profession into a mission, sharing the untold stories of the Thar Desert's unique ecosystem with wildlife enthusiasts and nature photographers from around the world. His extraordinary dedication to Great Indian Bustard conservation has been recognized by Sanctuary Asia, earning him prestigious awards and a leadership role in the Mud-on-Boots Project. Through relentless efforts to protect the Great Indian Bustard and chinkara (Indian gazelle) populations, Radhe has become a celebrated figure in wildlife conservation circles, with his desert safari tours and conservation work gaining traction on social media and at notable award ceremonies.


From a humble village boy nursing injured animals to an emblem of hope and resilience in Thar Desert wildlife conservation, Radhe exemplifies the harmony that can exist between humans and the desert ecosystem. Beyond his work in eco-tourism and wildlife safaris in Jaisalmer, he stands as a beacon for sustainable desert tourism and biodiversity preservation. Radhe's inspiring story as the "Godawan Man" (named after the local term for Great Indian Bustard) is a testament to turning conservation challenges into triumphs, leaving an indelible mark not only on the Desert National Park but also on the hearts of all wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers who encounter his journey.
